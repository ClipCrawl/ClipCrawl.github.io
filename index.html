AMBER

Measuring the ambiguity penalty in corpus-level video moment retrieval. A benchmark that preserves the whole match set — every match edge verified on pixels — plus the agentic verification method built on top of it, and the honest negative results.

1,106 queries 3 video corpora every edge pixel-verified penalty +19.9 @1 on ambiguous queries

The problem

Retrieval scores lie when the query is ambiguous.

35distinct videos in our corpus genuinely contain a moment matching one real query — “a person enters a room through a door.” One is marked gold; 34 correct answers get penalized.

Every widely-used corpus-level VCMR benchmark marks exactly one gold moment per query. But queries are rarely that specific. A system that returns any of the non-annotated true matches is scored wrong for being right — so reported accuracy is a lower bound of unknown tightness. AMBER makes that looseness measurable.

The dataset

1,106 queries, and every match they have.

Built from three public corpora into one ambiguity graph per query, then projected into two evaluation tracks. Construction runs entirely on open-weight local models.

The two tracks

U — query-unique

Honest single-gold

558 queries whose verified match set is a single moment. Standard R@K / VCMR@IoU are exact here — there is no un-annotated true match to wrongly penalize.

C — recall-complete

The full match set

All 1,106 queries with every verified match published. Multi-positive metrics (Correct@K, mAP@R, NDCG@IoU): a system is right if it finds any true moment.

Live examples — real records, real frames

Label quality, stated plainly. Captions, queries, entailment and the pixel gate share a model family, so we audit from outside it: an independent-family caption audit (1.00 / 0.91 / 0.94), a cross-family re-gate (InternVL3 confirms 89.8% of shipped edges; scan-mined edges are the weakest stratum at 82–85%), and a measured construction-bias probe — BM25 over the construction captions reaches 53–80% C-track@1, far above every pixel system, so the captions are withheld from the evaluation release. A human edge audit is the remaining step and is planned.
The measurement

One ranking, scored two ways.

The gap between them is the ambiguity penalty — the rate at which single-gold scoring marks a correct retrieval wrong.

Single-gold scores barely move while honest scores climb: the better the system, the more often its top-1 is a true-but-unannotated match — single-gold leaderboards compress exactly the differences they exist to measure (gap vs CLIP: +9.1, CI [1.1, 18.2]). A corpus-moment-level analogue of pooling bias in IR evaluation.

Construction — caption-driven, pixel-gated

How a record is built — every stage, with the real artifacts.

Two principles. Captions are the working medium: text is cheap to search, so candidate mining happens in caption space. Pixels are the authority: no edge ships unless a VLM re-confirms it on frames from the actual span — anchors included, fail-closed. One local Qwen3-VL-8B serves every role (caption, query-gen, merge judge, scan judge, pixel gate), fully offline on a single A100 — 8.1 h for all of Charades-FIG. Every artifact below is verbatim from the released run files.

The Charades-FIG funnel (one of three sources, real counts)
933 videos→ 2,391 events→ 4,754 queries → 1,200 after merge→ +2,892 scan edges → pixel gate −9.3% edges, −31 anchors→ 1,169 records → 375 stratified into v1

Stage 0 · event segmentation — calibrated change-points

mechanism

Cosine dip below a per-source calibrated threshold

Consecutive-frame CLIP similarity dips below a threshold ⇒ boundary. The threshold is not hand-picked: bisection over [0.5, 0.999] targets a median event length of 8–12 s (min 4 s merged, max 60 s split). Charades-FIG calibrated to thr=0.874, median 8.4 s. Each event then gets 3–8 interior frames (n = ceil(dur/4), clipped to [3,8]) — the same frames every later stage will see.

example · video 735W9 → three events

One 30-second Charades clip becomes three events. e2 — the hairdryer moment — is the anchor we follow to release below.

Stage 1 · captioning — the sole semantic medium

mechanism

Qwen3-VL-8B, one caption per event

Prompt rules: 1–2 sentences of action description; read on-screen text before guessing; never invent brand or device names; no speculation. 512-px thumbnails of the 3–8 interior frames. Captions are audited (100-sample faithfulness: 0.96 raw round-1, 1.0 after recheck; later an independent-family judge confirms 89.8% of shipped edges) and are withheld from the evaluation release — systems never see them.

example · the three captions, verbatim

e0 — “A person in a pink hoodie and glasses holds a large, dark-colored cylindrical object with a blue section, rotating it while seated at a wooden table.”
e1 — “A person in a pink hoodie and glasses sits at a table, holding and examining a black and blue handheld device, likely a handheld vacuum or similar appliance, while looking down at it.”
e2 — “A person in a pink hoodie and glasses is seated at a wooden table, holding and manipulating a black and blue hairdryer, rotating it in their hands.”

Note the captioner hedging on e1 (“likely a handheld vacuum”) — exactly the ambiguity the pixel gate and cross-family audit exist to police.

Stage 2 · query generation — anti-parroting, measured

anchor eventgranularitygenerated query (verbatim)trigram overlap

≤1 fine + ≤1 coarse query per event. Fine keeps discriminative details (appearance, place, manner, order); coarse keeps only the action skeleton. The anti-parroting control is two-layer and honest about being soft: the prompt bans reusing 3 consecutive caption words, and every query’s measured trigram-overlap ratio (fraction of query word-trigrams appearing verbatim in the caption) is stored; queries above 0.6 are hard-rejected. Corpus mean 0.108 — the 0.40 row above shows the control is a measured ceiling, not a guarantee of zero, which is why the number ships with every query.

Stage 3 · merge — ambiguity found before searching

mechanism

Same query from different videos ⇒ one record

Sentence-embedding neighbors (cos ≥ 0.88, same granularity, top-10 peers per query) go to an LLM equivalence judge — “same kind of moment, same constraints?” A failed judgment counts as not-same: the pipeline under-merges rather than hallucinating equivalence. Union-find components collapse 4,754 raw queries into 1,200 records (235 pair judgments on Charades-FIG); absorbed anchors enter the match set tagged mined_by:"merge" — and still face the pixel gate like every other edge.

why it matters

Merge is discovery, not deduplication

When two events in different videos independently generate the same query, that coincidence is evidence the query is ambiguous — the match set gains a candidate edge before any search happens. The scan below is the recall workhorse; merge catches the pairs text search can miss because they are phrased identically.

Stage 4 · corpus scan — directional entailment over every caption

mechanism

BM25 ∪ embedding shortlist → “does it SATISFY the query?”

Each query retrieves a k=200 shortlist (BM25 and sentence-embedding search, merged by rank) over all corpus captions; up to 40 unseen candidates get a directional entailment judgment — “does the described segment satisfy the query” — explicitly not topical similarity. Acceptance is the judge’s boolean; confidences are carried through as diagnostics, not thresholds. Depth sensitivity: edge count plateaus at depth 50 (2,892 edges at k=50/100/200) — the shortlist is not the recall bottleneck.

example · what the judge actually read

query (coarse, from XJA7Z_e0): “Someone drinks from a bottle then lowers it and touches their mouth.”

A true match at shortlist rank 33 — this is why the scan judges deep into the list instead of trusting retrieval order.

Stage 5 · the pixel gate — gate-all, fail-closed

mechanism

Every edge re-verified on its own frames — anchors included

The VLM re-checks each surviving edge against 3–8 frames sampled from that edge’s span. Full runs gate everything: anchors, merge edges, scan edges. A rejected anchor drops its whole record — the caption that generated the query was unfaithful, so the query itself is suspect. Two failure counters are kept apart: n_visual_rejected (judge said no) and n_verify_failed (anything threw — dropped fail-closed, never shipped unverified).

what it removed (Charades-FIG, real)

9.3% of edges cut · 31 whole records dropped

The five drinking-moments above all passed (visual_verified:true). Across the source, the gate cut 9.3% of caption-space edges and rejected 31 anchors outright; verify_failed=0 — nothing shipped on a crashed check. Post-release, a cross-family perceiver (InternVL3, non-Qwen) confirms 89.8% of a 300-edge stratified sample. Honest caveat, disclosed: judge confidences cluster at 0.95 and are not calibrated — the gate’s authority is the boolean re-check plus the independent audits, not the number.

assembly rules

Tracks, splits, safety flags

ambiguity_size = distinct verified events. Records with one verified moment carry tracks ["U","C"] (single-gold metrics are honest on them); multi-moment records are ["C"]-only. Splits are video-disjoint on the anchor’s video (60/15/25); train records additionally carry supervision_safe — true iff none of the record’s match-set videos appear in any val/test match set (enforced by a released unit test; this flag is what caught our own SFT leakage). v1 then stratifies 375 records per source across granularity × unique/ambiguous. The U-track sibling of this walkthrough — pilot_00022, “A person sits at a table and rotates a hairdryer.”, match set = exactly 735W9 [10.2,20.4] — ships with ambiguity_size:1, tracks:["U","C"].

Known limit, disclosed rather than hidden: match sets are mined in caption space, so they are caption-reachable by construction — our BM25 probe quantifies this and the natural-query control shows the ambiguity is a property of the videos, not of our generated phrasing.

Memory design

RepoMem — verification memory over the ambiguity graph.

Single-video memory agents (WorldMM-style) cache perception — captions, triples, snapshots — inside one long video. RepoMem is the corpus-level counterpart: it caches verified decisions across queries, with trust dynamics, and its reuse decisions are the first that a benchmark can actually score.

The memory entry (query-conditional — the load-bearing choice)

entry schema
{
  "moment":  ("charades_fig", "735W9", 10.2),
  "query_text": "A person sits at a table and
                 rotates a hairdryer.",
  "query_emb":  <MiniLM vector>,
  "verdict":  "match" | "reject",   // rejects cached too
  "conf":     0.85,
  "provenance": "pixel" | "reused",
  "contradicted": false
}
the three rules

R1 · depth-1 reuse. Only pixel-verified entries are reusable; entries born from reuse never are — error avalanches are impossible by construction.

R2 · negative cache. A look-alike rejected for one query class is not re-inspected for a near-duplicate query.

R3 · contradiction ⇒ reopen. A fresh pixel verdict contradicting a stored same-class entry poisons both — the next encounter re-verifies on pixels.

The reuse gate

Reuse a stored verdict for a new query q′ on moment m only if: same moment · query similarity ≥ τq · pixel provenance, uncontradicted · confidence ≥ τconf. The gateless ablation (moment identity alone) is the harm arm; an oracle arm (reuse exactly along ground-truth edges) bounds what the protocol can offer. Verdicts are query-conditional — the same moment can be a match for “rotates a hairdryer” and a non-match for “opens a fridge” — which is why a bare moment→verdict cache would be wrong.

Why the ambiguity graph makes memory measurable

On the test stream there are 1,600 reuse opportunities (763 moments shared across queries' candidate lists). Each reuse event is scoreable offline: a reused match on moment m for query q′ is correct iff m ∈ Mq′ — ground truth no other benchmark publishes. Pre-registered evaluation (OFF / ON / GATELESS / ORACLE × 5 stream orders, τ-sweep frontier) runs as exact CPU simulation over one cached GPU pass of verifier scores; results land here when the score-dump job clears the cluster queue.

Negative results, reported as such

SFT distillation: null. An apparent +2.7 gain under a naive anchor-disjoint split was entirely supervision leakage (39% of test queries had a gold video the student trained on). After decontamination: −0.7 vs zero-shot (p=0.83). We ship the decontaminated numbers and a per-record supervision_safe flag.
Multi-positive RL reward vs anchor-only: null — twice. A first run was invalidated by design review (zero-gradient groups, rewards differing on only 11% of queries). The corrected rerun — adversarially reviewed, gold events injected into training candidates, a recall-complete eval where the contrast is expressible — still finds no difference (multi-video paired sign test +7/−5, p=0.77), and no suppression signature: anchor-reward training picks non-anchor true matches as often as C-track training (37.5% vs 39.0%). At 3B/LoRA scale with 357 clean queries, reward semantics do not measurably matter.
Granularity dependence: not supported. Agent gains on fine vs coarse queries (+14.4 vs +11.2) are within noise (z=0.52). The agent helps significantly on both.

Limitations

Match sets are complete only relative to the released pools (199–933 videos/source); a human edge audit and the Charades-STA natural-query control are planned; the frontier-agent arm uses a proprietary assistant (prompt and all 259 trajectories released); MOMA was excluded after failing the caption-faithfulness gate at every captioner setting tried (0.81–0.87) — a documented VLM ceiling on sports on-screen text.
